Welcome to Budva

Visit Budva Top Spots

In the embrace of the Adriatic's azure waves lies Budva, a timeless jewel shimmering with allure. Wander through the ancient cobblestone sreets of the Old Town. Bask in the golden embrace of Mogren beach. Whether you are a history buff or beach lover, Budva promises an unforgettable experience.

History

Budva, one of the oldest settlements on the Adriatic coast, boasts a rich history that dates back to the 5th century BC. Initially settled by the Illyrians, an ancient Indo-European people, Budva's strategic location on a rocky peninsula made it a vital trading hub. During the Greek colonization of the Adriatic in the 6th century BC, a Greek emporium was established in the area. By the 2nd century BC, Budva became part of the Roman Republic and later the Roman Empire, flourishing as a bustling center of trade and culture. The remnants of Roman villas, walls, and fortifications still visible today are a testament to its significance during this era.

Throughout the Middle Ages, Budva experienced a series of power shifts, coming under the influence of the Byzantine Empire, Slavic tribes, and various medieval states. The Venetian Republic's arrival in the 15th century marked a significant chapter in Budva's history, with the Venetians ruling for around 400 years. They expanded the city's fortifications, leaving a lasting impact on its architecture, evident in the elegant palaces, churches, and squares that define the Old Town. Budva's history is also marked by brief periods of Ottoman and Habsburg rule, each leaving their mark on the city's cultural fabric.

Culture

Budva, a coastal gem in Montenegro, boasts a rich cultural tapestry woven through centuries of history. The town's Old Town, or Stari Grad, is a maze of narrow, cobbled streets lined with medieval stone houses, ancient churches, and a fortress that offers stunning views of the Adriatic Sea. This area is a testament to Budva's long history and includes influences from the Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, and Venetians. The architectural marvels, such as the Citadel and the Church of St. Ivan, reflect this diverse heritage and provide a picturesque backdrop for visitors exploring the town.

Budva's culture is also vibrant and lively, with numerous festivals and events that celebrate its rich traditions and artistic expressions. The Budva Summer Festival, for instance, features music, theater performances, and art exhibitions, drawing crowds from near and far. Additionally, the International Carnival brings the streets to life with colorful parades and a festive atmosphere. These events, along with the town's delicious local cuisine and welcoming atmosphere, offer a deep connection to Budva's heritage and make it a captivating destination for those looking to immerse themselves in Montenegro's coastal culture.
